# Lens Design Portfolio

## Reflective and Catadioptric Design Studies

Exploration of reflective and catadioptric systems, focusing on wide-field performance and manufacturing metrology.

- **Hubble Space Telescope (RC Aplanat):** Optimized mirror conic constants to achieve aplanatic, diffraction-limited performance.
- **Null Test Metrology:** Comparative design of Dall and Offner compensators** for high-precision testing of aspheric primary mirrors.
- **Catadioptric Cameras:** Performance trade-offs between Schmidt, Maksutov, and Houghton** systems for wide-field survey applications.
- **Schupmann Medial Telescope:** Design of an all-BK7 apochromatic system utilizing a Mangin mirror for full color correction.

## Photographic Lens 

Analysis and optimization of classic refractive objectives, tracing the development of field correction and resolution.

- **Objective Comparisons:** Performance studies of Landscape, Petzval, and Cooke Triplet designs.
- **Double Gauss Objective:** Advanced optimization of a symmetric architecture to successfully meet industry MTF 30/50 benchmarks.
- **Portrait Lens with Field Flattener:** Implementation of a negative lens element near the image plane to correct Petzval curvature and distortion.

## Microscope and Relay Systems

Design of high-precision, small-scale imaging systems and magnification-stable relays.

- **High-NA Objective:** A 7-element, f/1 (NA 0.5) microscope objective achieving diffraction-limited performance (< 0.07-wave RMS).
- **System Integration:** Development of a 150x microscope relay assembly combining objectives with optimized focusing groups.
- **Bi-Telecentric Relays:** Design of m=−1 and m=−2 imaging relays to maintain magnification stability for metrology.

## Primary and Chromatic Aberration Control

Fundamental studies in high-order aberration balancing and chromatic focal shift minimization.

- **Apochromatic Design:** Utilization of three glass types (FPL53, NBK7, NBAF52) to eliminate the secondary spectrum in aplanatic triplets.
- **Precision Optimization:** Use of buried surfaces, aspheric coefficients, and lens bending to eliminate spherical aberration and coma.
- **Achromatization Techniques:** Comparison of crown-in-front vs. flint-in-front doublet architectures.
