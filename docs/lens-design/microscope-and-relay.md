# Microscope and Relay Systems


# High-NA F/1 Microscope Objective

- **Specs:** f=1 mm, F/1 (NA≈0.5 equivalent), telecentric image space, <2% distortion.
- **Design Approach:** Utilized a 7-element architecture starting from a modified Petzval objective base. Corrected spherical aberration using a negative meniscus and managed field curvature by distributing optical power across PSK52 and SF6 doublets.
- **Outcome:** Achieved an RMS wavefront error of **0.07 waves**, reaching diffraction-limited performance across the full field.

# 150x Microscope Relay Assembly

- **Specs:** 150x magnification system with near-diffraction limited performance.
- **Design Approach:** Integrated the F/1 objective in a reversed configuration with an optimized f=150 mm triplet focusing lens.
- **Outcome:** Leveraged system-wide telecentricity to ensure that magnification remained stable despite telecentric imperfections, resulting in a **1.2% magnification accuracy**.
  ![](../images/Pasted image 20260304110608.png)

# Bi-Telecentric Imaging Relays (m=−1 and m=−2)

- **Specs:** Unit magnification and 2x magnification imaging systems.
- **Design Approach:** Created symmetric pairings of telecentric Cooke triplets in a **front-to-front** configuration. For the m=−2 version, the second triplet was scaled by a factor of 2.
- **Outcome:** Achieved bi-telecentricity (both object and image space), making the system ideal for metrology applications where object movement must not affect magnification.

# Unit Magnification Pupil Relay

- **Specs:** 1:1 relay for pupil plane transfer.
- **Design Approach:** Combined two telecentric triplets in a **back-to-back** configuration.
- **Outcome:** Effectively transferred the pupil plane while maintaining chief ray angles, demonstrating the ability to manage complex pupil relay architectures in larger optical systems.
